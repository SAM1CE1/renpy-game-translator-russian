Why people move to PRO

When Base stops feeling like enough

Base is often enough to test a game and start reading. People move to PRO later, once one of these points starts getting in the way for real.

The meaning is there, but the scene still sounds off

In Base: With Base, you understand what is happening and can keep reading.

What starts to bother you: But important scenes can still feel flatter, drier, or simply wrong for the mood.

What PRO changes: Context-aware AI keeps nearby lines, subtext, and scene tone in view.

Why that becomes worth paying for: That is where you stop wanting basic understanding and start wanting the scene to read properly.

Dialogue is in Russian, but the game still feels split

In Base: The story is readable because the dialogue already translates.

What starts to bother you: Menus, history, save screens, and utility UI still keep breaking immersion in English.

What PRO changes: PRO reaches further into the interface and makes the whole playthrough feel more coherent.

Why that becomes worth paying for: In a long VN, that becomes noticeable very quickly.

Weak lines start getting on your nerves

In Base: Translation works and the scene remains readable.

What starts to bother you: But awkward wording piles up and wears down long dialogue scenes.

What PRO changes: With PRO, you can fix the line inside the game and save the better version right away.

Why that becomes worth paying for: That is no longer about features. It is about reading comfort.

Time to decide on the upgrade

Signs that PRO is already worth it for you

A quick self-check before you upgrade. If several of these already sound like your game, PRO usually does not need much more explanation.

  • You already tried Base and want a steadier result.

    The story is understandable, but you want more than simply pushing forward through uneven lines.

  • You care about tone, not only bare meaning.

    This shows up most in emotional scenes where flat wording starts weakening the whole moment.

  • You read long VNs and weak lines start getting irritating.

    That is easy to tolerate in a short test. It becomes much harder over a long play session.

  • You want Russian in the interface too, not only in dialogue.

    When menus, history, and save screens stay in English, the game feels less complete.

  • You want to fix weak parts immediately instead of putting up with them.

    If you already feel the urge to correct lines during the scene, control over translation already matters to you.

If this already sounds like your situation

PRO will likely feel less like extra features and more like a calmer, better-controlled way to keep reading.
